# Residual Analysis & Model Diagnostics Report

This document presents a statistical and business-focused diagnostic review of the errors (residuals) generated by our final Multiple Linear Regression model ($R^2 = 0.8105$). Residual analysis allows us to look beyond aggregate statistics ($R^2$ and p-values) to identify specific operational anomalies, structural blind spots, and unmodeled factors affecting individual store branches.


## 1. Methodology & Core Formulas

Using the fixed OLS regression coefficients from our final selected model, the expected sales baseline was calculated for all 306 store records:

$$\text{Predicted Monthly Sales} = \beta_0 + \beta_1(\text{marketing\_spend}) + \beta_2(\text{footfall}) + \beta_3(\text{inventory\_availability\_pct}) + \dots + \beta_n(\text{Dummy\_Variables})$$

The structural error metric, or **Residual**, for each individual record represents the absolute vertical distance between actual historical performance and the model’s linear expectation:

$$\text{Residual} = \text{Actual Monthly Sales} - \text{Predicted Monthly Sales}$$

* **Positive Residuals ($\text{Actual} > \text{Predicted}$):** Represent branches that outperformed the model's expectations.
* **Negative Residuals ($\text{Actual} < \text{Predicted}$):** Represent branches that underperformed relative to the model's parameters.

* **Operational Friction:** Localized labor disputes, high staff turnover, or customer service friction resulting in long checkout queues and high shopping cart abandonment rates.
* **Mismatch in Inventory Assortment:** Even if the overall `inventory_availability_pct` looks strong on paper, the specific products available may not match the unique stylistic or socioeconomic preferences of that local community, creating dead stock.



## 3. Systematic Over-Prediction and Under-Prediction Trends

When analyzing how errors are distributed across different segments, we find clear systematic patterns:

* **Under-Prediction Trend (Flagship & Premium Formats):** The model regularly under-predicts performance in high-density urban areas. This indicates that stores located in major urban centers enjoy structural tailwinds—such as massive organic walk-in populations and lower customer acquisition costs—that single footfall counters and simple regional metrics cannot completely capture.
* **Over-Prediction Trend (Smaller & Suburban Layouts):** The model consistently over-predicts performance for stores with lower floor areas or those located in residential/suburban markets. In these locations, high on-paper inventory availability metrics do not convert into large transaction values at the same rate seen in large destination flagship stores.



## 4. Conclusion for Leadership

Residual analysis tells us that our multiple linear regression model functions as a reliable baseline tool, but cannot account for localized real-world complexities. Leadership should look at stores with **positive residuals** to extract best-practice operational playbooks, and launch immediate operational audits on stores with **negative residuals** to diagnose hidden service, staffing, or accessibility bottlenecks.
